# Nine World Aspects

Use the nine aspects as narrative functions, not as a checklist of objects. Generate all nine in order by default; obey a user-requested subset or count.

## 1. Inhabitants / 居民

Reveal who lives here through role, posture, body covering, behavior, and relationship to the environment. Allow human, non-human, altered, mechanical, ancestral, mythical, animal-like, or spirit-like inhabitants. Never produce a lineup, fashion pose, character sheet, or species display.

## 2. Animals / 动物

Reveal what other life shares the world and how it relates to inhabitants: labor, care, migration, hunting, worship, fear, riding, following, nesting, tracks, breath, shadow, or remains. Never produce a creature sheet or specimen display.

## 3. Architecture / 建筑

Reveal where beings shelter, gather, rule, hide, repair, worship, or remember. Show built space in use through smoke, footsteps, fabric, cooking, tools, passage, damage, maintenance, guarding, or ritual. Never produce an empty design render.

## 4. Landscape / 地景

Treat geography, climate, and terrain as forces shaping civilization. Show scale through distant figures, routes, erosion, crops, ruins, boats, smoke, flags, footprints, or weather. Do not use land as passive scenery.

## 5. Daily Life / 日常

Show an ordinary repeated action: labor, food preparation, craft, washing, repair, trade, family rhythm, training, play, tending fire, or preparing tools. Keep it ordinary rather than ceremonial, catastrophic, combative, or symbolically posed.

## 6. Migration and Transport / 迁徙与交通

Show how beings, goods, or knowledge cross space through walking, riding, rowing, dragging, climbing, drifting, departing, or arriving. Make distance, route, terrain, weight, rhythm, and effort visible. Describe motion frozen in a still; do not write video camera movement.

## 7. Sound and Culture / 声音与文化

Make sound, memory, meaning, or performance visible through listening, chanting, bells, instruments, tools striking material, dance, wind-driven objects, silence, oral tradition, mourning, ceremony, or song.

## 8. Power and Pressure / 权力与压力

Reveal what tests or governs the world: ritual authority, social silence, dangerous labor, scarcity, heat, storm, flood, punishment, pursuit, confrontation, or the instant before change. Do not default to battle.

## 9. Intimate Portrait / 私密肖像

Reveal the world's soul through one private emotional beat. Use reaction, gesture, glance, wound, breath, dirty hands, a half-lit face, a hidden expression, or off-frame listening. Do not default to a centered front-facing portrait.

## Resolve overlap

- Routine belongs to Daily Life.
- Crossing space belongs to Migration and Transport.
- Sound, memory, or performance belongs to Sound and Culture.
- Crisis, authority, or pressure belongs to Power and Pressure.
- A private reaction belongs to Intimate Portrait.
- Land acting as a force belongs to Landscape.
- Built space shown through use belongs to Architecture.
