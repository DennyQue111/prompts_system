## Description
Use this architecture when generating an image focused on a location or environment — landscapes, interiors, cityscapes, fantasy realms. The structure prioritizes spatial composition, mood, and environmental storytelling.

## Prompt Formula

```
[Location type] + [Geography & spatial layout] + [Architecture & structural elements] +
[Time & weather conditions] + [Atmosphere & mood] + [Lighting & color palette] +
[Detail accents] + [Style suffix]
```

### Key Components

| Component | What It Covers |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Location type** | The fundamental category: "a misty mountain temple", "a neon-drenched cyberpunk street" |
| **Geography & spatial layout** | Scale, depth, terrain, spatial relationships: "nestled in a steep valley", "a narrow alley between towers" |
| **Architecture & structure** | Building style, materials, key structures: "weathered stone arches", "holographic billboards and chrome walkways" |
| **Time & weather** | Time of day, season, weather: "golden hour", "midnight under full moon", "fog rolling in from the sea" |
| **Atmosphere & mood** | Emotional quality: "serene and sacred", "ominous and claustrophobic", "lonely and abandoned" |
| **Lighting & color** | Dominant light sources and color temperature: "warm amber lanterns against cool blue twilight", "harsh neon pink and cyan" |
| **Detail accents** | Small storytelling elements: "prayer flags fluttering", "steam vents hissing from grates", "vines cracking the stone floor" |
| **Style suffix** | Rendering quality + art style. Derive from project visual direction. Concept sheets use lighter specs than final cinematic shots — no camera brand names. |

## Key Rules

1. **Focal anchor**: Every location needs a visual anchor — a building, a light source, a landmark — around which the composition orbits. Wide landscapes without an anchor fade into generic blobs.
2. **Time of day is non-negotiable**: Always specify it. "A forest" → "an ancient pine forest at dawn, mist on the forest floor, golden rays piercing the canopy."
3. **Atmosphere > object inventory**: Lighting and mood drive location quality more than listing every object. Use "detail accents" to sprinkle narrative flavor, not catalog everything in the scene.
4. **For interiors**: Include ceiling height and room scale to avoid cramped AI compositions.
5. **Avoid list-like descriptions**: Don't enumerate objects. Integrate them into the spatial narrative. "A church" → "a crumbling Gothic church, blackened stone, jagged spires silhouetted against a blood-red sunset."
